Europe

My absolute favorite. You can’t help falling in love with the architecture, culture, people and the overall chill vibe.

Amsterdam

My favorite city in the world! I keep on coming back there (9 times and counting), because it simply makes me the happiest. Here are my top recommendations:

Places to eat:

  • Van der Linde - Incredible ice cream (some(=me) may say it's the best in the world).
  • Poffertjes Albert Cuyp - The best poffertjes in town. Don’t settle for less!
  • Café 't Smalle - A cute café on a beautiful canal, make sure to try to sit outside.

What to see:

  • Jordaan - My favorite neighborhood in the city, what comes in my mind when you think of picturesque Amsterdam.
  • Bloemgracht - The most beautiful canal (and it’s a tough competition).
  • De Pijp - A lively neighborhood with lots of bars and restaurants.

Haarlem

A cute city right next to Amsterdam, and one of my favorite cities to hang out in. I love a lot of places there, like:

Places to eat:

  • OSCAR - Cute bakery with an excellent almond croissant.
  • The Hive - A great Israeli (!) café with traditional Israeli food.
  • Coffee Habits Specialty Coffee - Good coffee and amazing sweet traits.

What to see:

  • Windmill De Adriaan - An impressive old windmill that has been beautifully preserved.
  • Zuid-Kennemerland National Park - Lovely nature reserve with deers!
  • Haarlem beaches - beautiful, sandy beaches just 15 minutes away from the city center.

Utrecht

Great, big city that not a lot of Isralis know. Here are my top recommendations:

Places to eat:

  • 30ml Vredenburg - The best place for breakfast or brunch.
  • Saowapa - Nice little Thai restaurant a little bit outside the city center.

What to see:

  • Canals - Utrecht has really unique looking canals! You should edicate at least a day to have a walk by them.
  • Pandhof van de Dom - I got there by mistake, and found a garden within a church that looks like Hogwarts!
  • Oudegracht - The main street that has a lot of cozy café, cute flower shops and great stores.

One day I’ll definitely return to the UK for destinations like Liverpool (where my family is from), Lake District and the Scottish Highlands. Here’s where I’ve been so far:

London

London does wait for you! It's such a vibrant city with rich history, iconic landmarks, and every type of food you can think of.

Places to eat:

  • Oree bakery - Chain with great coffee and the best chocolate croissant I’ve ever had.
  • The Alley - The best bubble tea I had in London (and I've tried a fair amount).
  • The Cheese Bar - Located in Camden Market, an entire restaurant dedicated to cheese. Do I need to say more?

What to see:

  • Warner Bros. Studio Tour - As a huge Harry Potter fan, it was a dream come true!
  • Puppy Yoga - A yoga class you're having while surrounded by a cheerful squad of puppies. It’s as amazing as it sounds.
  • Visiting beautiful streets - The prettiest streets I’ve found were Floral street, Goodwin’s court and Neal’s yard.

Edinburgh

The minute I stepped in Edinburgh, I fell in love. The view of the city is so unique and spectacular, and the people are so welcoming and nice. I highly recommend visiting this city!

Places to eat:

  • Hula Juice Bar & Healthy Eatery - Tasty breakfast options and refreshing juices.
  • The Milkman - One of the coziest cafes I’ve ever seen, located in a beautiful street corner.

What to see:

  • The alleys - Get lost in Edinburgh! everywhere you’ll turn, you’ll probably end up in an enchanting alley with rich history.
  • Calton Hill - Picturesque hill that offers panoramic views of the city. You should come at sunset!
  • Dean Village - A charming neighborhood that looks like a fairy village.

I had lots of fun in Budapest, Hungary's capital, when I traveled there!
The streets are charming, and every building has its unique architecture and pieces of art on it. You’ll probably end your day with neck pain from looking up so much, because you’ll want to see them all!
There are so many things to do and see there but my top recommendation is definitely Costes Downtown restaurant - a michelin star restaurant where I had the best meal i ever had in my entire life, for the price of 90 NIS for appetizer, main course and desert!

Rome

I’ll always have a warm place in my heart for Rome, because I traveled there the same day I finished my military service. I wish I could stay there for weeks and just keep on eating! divine ice cream, pizzas and pastas, and so many beautiful places to see.

Naples

A beautiful combination of European and Mediterranean cultures. But the real highlight of the city is of course the Neapolitan pizza. Definitely visit L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele, it’s worth the line. Protip: the line for the takeaway is shorter.

France has so many amazing places you can travel to, I can’t wait to explore them all! Here are my top places I've been so far:

Paris

The city of love and lights, and of course, the city of croissants :)

Places to eat:

  • La Maison d’Isabelle - Incredible bakery with a divine croissant, and many more delicious treats.
  • Cédric Grolet Opéra - A famous patisserie that’s definitely worth the (long) line.
  • Angelina - Their hot chocolate feels like a chocolatey dream!
  • THAIGER - Great Thai restaurant in the Latin quarter.
  • McDonald's - I’m sorry, but I've never had a better McFlurry.

What to see:

  • Garden of the Eiffel Tower - a great place to sit at night with a bottle of wine, watching the Eiffel tower sparkles.
  • Montmartre - Beautiful neighborhood with such a rich history.
  • The Seine - A walk along the Seine, starting from Pont Neuf, all the way to the Eiffel tower.
  • Canal Saint-Martin - Had so much fun eating my lunch right next to it!

Strasbourg and Colmar

Alsace, a fairy-tale looking area in East France, is a must! The area is famous for its fine wine and jams, and the beautiful towns and cities look like they were taken straight out of a Disney movie. Strasbourg, the capital of the area, is a really cute city with lots to see. I recommend spending at least a few days there. Colmar is an adorable small town right next to Strasbourg, if you’re around you should totally give it a visit!

Dijon

Great student city and the capital of Burgundy, with beautiful streets, great food, great wine and even great prices! Really recommended for a short stay on a budget, and if you like Dijon mustard :)

Annecy

The city is built around a beautiful, big lake and it has an amazing park, Jardins de l'Europe, where you can chill and look at the water. You can also visit Le Palais de I'Île, the famous building that’s right next to a cute river, and travel the lovely streets around it.

Vienna

When you think of a classical European city- Vienna comes to mind. Clean-looking buildings and streets, with lots of cafés that were open hundreds of years ago. I strongly recommend you to come at Christmas time- the markets and the vibe are amazing!

Tyrol and Innsbruck, SalzburgerLandes and Salzburg

Two amazing areas in Austria, filled with incredible mountains, lakes, waterfalls, gorges, amazing hiking variety, cute alpine houses, and breath-taking views.
There aren't many things that are better than eating a schnitzel on top of a 3,000 meter mountain!

I came back from Finland just a few hours before the first covid quarantine started, and boy, was I lucky I got to experience this country!
I was traveling in the north of Finland, in Lapland, And it was a true winter wonderland!
Tons of snow and snowy forests, frozen waterfalls, dogsled, reindeer sled, racing in a race track made out of snow, visiting the north pole and Santa's village, and of course, watching the northern lights (twice!).
If possible, I really recommend sleeping in a glass igloo - it’s such a unique experience!

Germany is such a big country with lots to see. Here are some places I visited and enjoyed:

Berlin

The city of uniqueness (and Milky). Here are my top choices:

Places to eat:

  • The Bird - Amazing burgers! pretty far from the center but it’s totally worth it.
  • Roamers - Great place for brunch, the pancakes are highly recommended!
  • House of Small Wonder - Japanese-inspired café with cute design and great food and drinks.

What to see:

  • Hackescher Markt - Fun area to explore with inner gardens and art.
  • Klunkerkranich - The entire city is spread in front of you, while you can listen to live shows!
  • Stadtgericht - Looks like a pretty ordinary building from the outside, but once you get in you’ll find beautiful architecture!

Baden Baden

Its name literally means “Bath Bath”. A great city for a short, relaxed getaway that’s in the middle of the black forest. It has some really nice Baths (the name of the city implies that…) and it’s really nice to walk along the beautiful river that crosses the city. A really cool thing you can do there is a race track where you can drive a Porsche, Lamborghini or another fancy sports car. Afterwards, the car is yours for the day.

South of Germany

Right next to the border with Austria, Germany has amazing places to hike and travel. Lakes like Königssee and Walchensee are amazing for a nice visit to nature.

In Spain I've only been in Barcelona, but staying there, I already fell in love with the Spanish culture. I Celebrated new year’s eve in Barcelona, and it was so much fun, they really know how to party!
I highly recommend visiting there, seeing the unique architecture and tasting the incredible food.

America

I spent a month and a half in Peru and it was an absolute blast. I had so many fun moments there and I made memories that warm my heart everytime I think of them. The culture, the breathtaking views of the Andes, and of course, the Machu Picchu, all made my time in Peru such an amazing experience! Here are some of the places I visited:

Cusco

My favorite city in Peru. Surrounded by beautiful nature and lots of things to see, do and explore, so you’ll never get bored! From there you can travel to Machu Picchu and the Rainbow Mountain. It also has an incredible culinary scene, my favorites were Café Dwasi and La Bodega 138.

Valle Sagrado

Beautiful rural area. A green valley with small towns, where you can find yoga classes and vegan food, next to incredible Inca sites.

Huaraz

The city is known for being a base for trekking. While staying there, I visited Laguna Paron, Laguna 69, Pastoruri glacier and did an 8-days trek, the Huayhuash trail.

Huacachina

A true oasis! A cute town with a lake in the middle of the desert, where you can take a dune buggy tour and sandboarding.

It was my last destination in my big trip before university, and it was a great destination to finish my trip with! The amazing beaches and jungles there really eased my soul and made me feel very calm and happy. Here are some places I’ve enjoyed:

Panama City

Cool city with stunning skyline, and a lot of things to do. I enjoyed walking around and eating at local cafés and American chains (it was my first time trying Krispy Kreme and I loved it!).

Bocas del Toro Archipelago

Amazing islands that combine stunning beaches with abundant jungles. My favorite islands were Bastimentos island (red frog island), Zapatilla island and Colón island (the main island).

El Valle de Anton

Beautiful mountain town with lots of nature around and a great variety of hikes.

San Blas Islands

I was staying on one of the islands for 2 days- and it was completely isolated! It was just me and 4 more people and one local family, with no reception or functioning utilities. I was there by myself and all I did was swim in the turquoise sea or read a book at the beach. It was so relaxing!

In Ecuador I had the most amazing experience I’ve ever had- discovering the underwater world in The Galapagos islands.
Every time I entered the water I felt like i’m in a dream - tons of colorful fish, giant turtles, starfish, sea lions and so many more exotic animals.
I even got to swim with sharks, and saw a whale and some penguins!
